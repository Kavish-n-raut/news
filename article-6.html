Energy Stocks in Focus as Crude Slide Boosts Refining Margins

Energy stocks were at the center of market attention today, with several key names witnessing notable movement after crude oil prices registered a sharp overnight decline. The drop in global crude benchmarks provided a direct boost to downstream players, improving expectations for refining and marketing margins across the sector.

Reliance Industries analysts noted that lower crude prices typically support the company's integrated refining and petrochemical businesses, offering a short-term uplift to profitability. Sentiment also improved around its oil-to-chemicals operations, which tend to benefit when feedstock costs ease.

ONGC also advanced more than 2%, though for a slightly different set of reasons. While lower crude prices generally weigh on upstream earnings, the market viewed the recent decline as temporary and instead focused on ONGC's broader production guidance and steady operational updates. The stock saw healthy buying interest from domestic institutions, who expect stability in the company's long-term revenue trajectory.

In contrast, Power Grid Corporation slipped after news emerged about delays in certain transmission projects. Concerns around execution timelines and potential cost overruns pressured the stock, offsetting some of the sector's gains. Despite mixed moves, energy remained one of the most actively traded segments of the session.
